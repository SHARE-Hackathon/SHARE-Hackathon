Welcome to the

March 2022


Come meet the next generation of IBM Z professionals, network with your peers, and share your own knowledge while getting hands-on exposure to the future of IBM Z applications. Register now to reserve a seat, be eligible to earn a badge, and stay informed about exclusive networking and hacking events taking place during the conference. This event will be a bring-your-own-computer event.

This event introduces you to the world of enterprise computing. Learn all about the strengths and capabilities of enterprise infrastructure when used in a hybrid cloud solution. During this two-day event you will learn how to code and interact with a live IBM Z mainframe through the full-stack development approach. You will also create code for a variety of applications including automating common administrative tasks and accessing data hosted on IBM Z using modern tooling and software.

FAQ


  • What is a Hackathon and how does it work? 
    • A hackathon, or hacking marathon, is an event where technologists of all experience levels and specialties come together to solve important problems by “hacking” together cool projects. At the end of a hackathon, you demo your project to a panel of judges for a chance to win prizes!
  • Who can participate in this event?
    • Anyone!
  • Where will this event be hosted?
    • The event will be hosted entirely online. We will use Discord as our main hub of communication. In addition to Discord text and voice channels, you may choose to collaborate with your team members using tools like Zoom or Webex. All submissions will be hosted on GitHub and demo videos on YouTube. 
  • What is the recommended size of a team?
    • Teams should have a minimum of 1 and a maximum of 5 members.
  • What if I don’t have a team?
    • We will be hosting a team building session in Discord immediately following the opening ceremony. If you do not have a team in mind, be sure to attend!
  • Can I start work on my project beforehand? 
    • No, you cannot start any part of your project before the start date. You may brainstorm ideas based on the event’s tracks and form a team beforehand, though.
  • What is my time commitment for this event?
    • Time commitment: The amount of time you commit is up to you. The more complex ideas will most likely require more time. Generally we expect teams to devote 8 hours of their time on a project. That's roughly an hour and ten minutes per day for the duration of the development part of the hackathon
  • I'm sold - how do I participate?
    • Gather your team, pick a project and a category, then register your team using the "Register" button above. Each team should only submit one entry.
  • What do I need to present?
    • Teams should prepare a 3 minute video presentation/demo - you may choose to prepare a slideshow, a demo or a combination. During your presentation, it should be clear what the challenge or problem you're trying to solve is, where you have innovated, what technologies you've employed, and finally the potential value if the project were to be carried forward.

      Videos should be uploaded to YouTube by 9pm CST, August 11th.
  • How do I prepare my presentation?

    • If you're presenting a slide show you may choose to use the "Record Slide Show" option from Microsoft PowerPoint and record your narration as you step through slides. Make sure to save these timing and narrations at the end and then export the PowerPoint as an MP4.

      Alternatively, if you're presenting a demo, Mac users may choose to use QuickTime to record a screen recording and Windows users may choose to use CamStudio which are both approved for use within G2O. Please make sure that your video includes clear audio before uploading.

  • What do I need to do to submit?

    • Teams should upload their prepared video presentation to YouTube. All code and associated artifacts should be committed and uploaded to your teams GitHub repo within the organization.

      https://github.com/SHARE-hackathon-Summer-2021
  • Which system should we be using for this event?

    • Instances/virtual environments with everything developers need to create, run and test their code and connectivity to an IBM Z system will be provided. Go to the Discord SHARE Hackathon channel to find instructions on connecting to the instances:

      Discord channel #general

  • What is the primary form of communication? - Discord

    • Our primary form of communication throughout the Hackathon will be Discord. This is where you will meet other hackers, connect with project liaisons for technical mentorship, and ask questions.

      Join the Discord server using this link:https://discord.gg/Af4yfm55H9

      If you have never used Discord before, check out this short tutorial video: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OIqyPJQAgT4 or Discord’s Beginner Guide:https://support.discord.com/hc/en-us/articles/360045138571-Beginner-s-Guide-to-Discord

      Please use your real name in Discord so we can grant you access to the appropriate channels for your role. If you need to update your Discord nickname, you can right click your name in the righthand sidebar and choose “Change Nickname” or use the /nick command followed by your desired name in a channel message. Changing your nickname in the SHARE Hackathon server will not change your nickname in other servers.

      Once you’re in Discord, check out the #welcome-and-rulesand #faq-and-schedulechannels for important information. Get help from Project Liaisons in #ask-a-liaisonor ask general logistical questions in #hackathon-questions. Use #generalfor friendly conversations with your fellow hackers!
